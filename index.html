About me

I’m Premanshu, a Master’s student in Electrical and Computer Engineering. I like learning systems end-to-end—hardware to software—and I enjoy building things that feel clear, predictable, and usable.

What I’m into

  • RTL design & computer architecture
  • Music (guitar), food, and geopolitics

Why accessibility matters to me

Accessibility is not "extra"—it’s part of quality. Small decisions (labels, focus order, readable layouts) can remove barriers and help more people participate independently.

My accessibility interests

I align with the social model of disability: many barriers come from environments and tools, not the person. I like changes that are concrete and testable.

Topics I want to explore more

  1. Keyboard navigation, visible focus, and predictable interaction
  2. Semantic HTML (headings/landmarks) and screen-reader-friendly layouts
  3. Clear language, captions, and reducing cognitive load

How I can contribute
