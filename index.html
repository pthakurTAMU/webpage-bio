About me

I’m Premanshu. I’m a second-year Master’s student in Computer Engineering. I enjoy learning systems end-to-end—from hardware to software—and I’m motivated by design that helps more people participate comfortably and independently.

What I’m into

  • Hardware/RTL fundamentals and verification
  • Automation and tooling with Python/Bash

Why accessibility matters to me

I believe accessibility is not “extra”—it’s part of quality. When we design with accessibility in mind, we reduce barriers and improve usability for everyone.

My accessibility interests

I’m especially aligned with the social model of disability: many difficulties people face come from environments and tools that weren’t designed inclusively. I like working on changes that are concrete and testable.

Topics I want to explore more

  1. Keyboard navigation, visible focus, and predictable interaction
  2. Semantic HTML (headings/landmarks) and screen-reader-friendly layouts
  3. Clear language, captions, and reducing cognitive load

How we could team up

If your interests overlap, I’d love to collaborate on a project where we can measure improvements (keyboard flow, clarity, error handling, etc.). I’m happy to help with testing, structure, and technical build-out.

Project directions I’d enjoy

  • Accessibility audit: evaluate a site and propose fixes with evidence
  • Form redesign: labels, instructions, errors, and keyboard-first UX
  • Captioning/usability check: compare experiences across devices and settings
